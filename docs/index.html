02 PREPARE THE BOOTABLE USB

Use Rufus to write the ISO to your USB drive.

*

Pro tip: Use Ventoy for a multiboot USB - load multiple ISOs on one drive and pick at boot. Essential for any technician.

For Windows 11, enable these Rufus bypass options:

rufus_options.cfg
  • Remove requirement for 4+ GB RAM, Secure Boot and TPM 2.0
  • Remove requirement for a Microsoft online account
  • Create a local account with a custom username
  • Disable data collection (skip privacy questions)
  • Disable automatic BitLocker device encryption

06 PHYSICAL TOOLS - OPENING & MAINTAINING PCs

For when you need to open the machine - not just fix it from software.

🔩 Essential Hand Tools

ToolWhy You Need ItBudget
Precision screwdriver kit (iFixit or 120+ bit generic)Phillips #00 #0 #1, Torx T5/T6/T8, Pentalobe for MacBooks. One kit covers every laptop and desktop.~$15-25
ESD anti-static wrist strapOne static discharge silently destroys RAM, SSDs, or GPUs. Costs $2-3 and saves components worth hundreds. Always wear it.~$2-5
Plastic spudgers and pry toolsOpen laptop chassis without scratching or snapping clips. Never use metal to separate plastic housings.~$5-10
Plastic opening picks (6-pack)Slide around the laptop edge to release clips gradually. Standard iFixit tool.~$5
Anti-static tweezersHandle small connectors, SMD components, and screws in tight spots.~$5-10
Magnetic screw tray or matLaptops have 20-40 screws of multiple sizes. Magnetic sections keep them organized by disassembly order.~$5-10
Suction cups (x2)Lift laptop screens and tablet glass on models with no visible opening gap.~$5

🌡️ Thermal Maintenance

ToolWhy You Need ItBudget
Thermal paste - Arctic MX-4 or MX-6 RECOMMENDEDReplace when reseating a CPU cooler or when a laptop overheats. Non-conductive, easy to apply, long lasting.~$7-10
Thermal paste - Noctua NT-H2Premium option. Marginally better performance, same process.~$12
Isopropyl alcohol 90-99% ESSENTIALRemove old thermal paste, clean PCB contacts. Must be high concentration - pharmacy 70% is too diluted.~$5-8
Lint-free wipes or coffee filtersApply IPA without leaving fibers. Cotton swabs for tight spots.~$3-5
Electric air blower (rechargeable) ESSENTIALCleans dust from heatsinks, fans, and keyboards. Refillable, consistent pressure. Way better than canned air.~$15-25
Soft anti-static brushGentle dust removal around capacitors where air pressure is too aggressive.~$3-5

⚡ Electrical Diagnostics

ToolWhy You Need ItBudget
ATX PSU tester with LCD display GAME CHANGERPlug all PSU connectors in, press a button, see +12V/+5V/+3.3V voltages with alarm if out of spec. Diagnoses a dead power supply in 30 seconds - no need to assemble the full PC.~$10-15
Digital multimeter (basic)Measure wall outlet voltage, test batteries, check cable continuity. A UT61 or any DT-830 generic covers all home needs.~$10-15
POST / debug card (PCIe)Plugs into a PCIe slot, shows a 2-digit hex code when the PC won't POST. Cross-reference with the motherboard manual to find which component failed before Windows even tries to load.~$5-10
RJ45 cable testerVerify an ethernet cable has continuity on all 8 pins. Essential if you suspect a bad cable.~$5-10

💻 Laptop-Specific Tools

ToolWhy You Need ItBudget
Heat gun or hair dryerSoften adhesive on modern laptops and tablets. Many Chromebooks, MacBooks, and slim models use glue instead of screws. Low heat, constant movement.~$15-30
USB to SATA adapter ESSENTIALConnect a removed HDD or SSD to another PC as an external drive. Critical for data recovery before formatting.~$10-15
CMOS battery - CR2032 (pack of 5)The coin cell that keeps BIOS settings alive. When the date resets on its own or the PC won't boot, this is usually the culprit. Keep a few in your drawer.~$3-5
Flashlight or headlampInside a PC case is dark. A headlamp keeps both hands free for the actual work.~$5-15

15 BROWSERS

The browser is the most used application on any computer - and the most dangerous vector for tracking, ads, and malware. Not all browsers are equal. This list is ordered from most recommended to least recommended.

!

First thing to install on any browser: uBlock Origin. No exceptions. It blocks ads, trackers, malware domains, and fingerprinting scripts. It's not just about comfort - it's a security tool. A machine without uBlock Origin is browsing unprotected.

✅ Best - Open Source, Privacy-Respecting

Firefox
★ Most Recommended
The only major browser that is 100% open source and not controlled by a surveillance company. Developed by the Mozilla Foundation (non-profit). Excellent extension support, strong privacy settings, and a huge community. Works with uBlock Origin perfectly. Install this as the default on every machine you touch.
mozilla.org/firefox
LibreWolf
★ Hardened Firefox
A fork of Firefox with all telemetry stripped out and privacy hardened by default. uBlock Origin comes pre-installed. No Mozilla accounts. No Pocket. No phone-home features. Ideal for users who want Firefox but with zero trust towards Mozilla's own data collection. For advanced users - some sites may break due to strict settings.
librewolf.net

👍 Good - Chromium-Based, Privacy-Conscious

Brave
Good Alternative
Built on Chromium but with Google's tracking stripped out. Includes a built-in ad blocker, fingerprint randomization, and optional Tor integration for private windows. Good choice for users who prefer a Chrome-like interface but want real privacy. Note: Brave has its own crypto/rewards system - you can ignore or disable it entirely.
brave.com
Ungoogled Chromium
For Power Users
Chromium (the open source base of Chrome) with all Google-specific code and calls removed. No account integration, no telemetry, no Google services. Closest to a clean, private Chrome experience. Installing extensions requires extra steps (no Chrome Web Store access by default).
ungoogled-software.github.io

⚠️ Use With Caution - Surveillance by Design

Google Chrome
Not Recommended
Chrome is the world's most popular browser - and Google's most powerful data collection tool. It tracks browsing history, login activity, location, and behavior across every website you visit, all of which feeds Google's advertising business. It also progressively limits the effectiveness of ad blockers (Manifest V3). If someone insists on using it, at minimum install uBlock Origin.
google.com/chrome
Microsoft Edge
Not Recommended
Like Chrome, Edge is Chromium-based but with Microsoft's own telemetry and services layered on top. It syncs data to Microsoft, pushes Bing and Copilot constantly, and was caught sending visited URLs to Microsoft servers without clear user consent. It's pre-installed on Windows and hard to avoid, but don't use it as the default browser.
Pre-installed on Windows

🔌 Essential Browser Extensions

Install these on Firefox or Brave. They work on all Chromium-based browsers too (Chrome, Edge, Brave, Ungoogled Chromium).

  • uBlock Origin - ublockorigin.com - Blocks ads, trackers, malware domains, and fingerprinting scripts. The single most important extension you can install. Open source. Uses almost no RAM. Works better than any paid antivirus at blocking malicious web content. MANDATORY
  • SponsorBlock - sponsor.ajay.app - Skips sponsor segments, intros, outros, and filler in YouTube videos automatically. Crowd-sourced database of time-stamped segments. Open source. Works in Firefox and all Chromium browsers. Saves hours of your life per month. OPEN SOURCE
  • Privacy Badger - privacybadger.org - Learns to block invisible trackers automatically. Made by the EFF (Electronic Frontier Foundation). Complements uBlock Origin by focusing on cross-site tracking rather than ad blocking. OPEN SOURCE
  • Bitwarden - bitwarden.com - The browser extension for the Bitwarden password manager. Autofills passwords, suggests strong ones, and warns you about phishing sites. Open source. OPEN SOURCE
  • ClearURLs - clearurls.xyz - Strips tracking parameters from URLs (UTM codes, fbclid, etc.) before you visit them or share them. Small and lightweight. OPEN SOURCE
  • Dark Reader - darkreader.org - Forces dark mode on every website. Useful for people who work long hours in front of a screen. Open source. Very configurable. OPEN SOURCE
  • Return YouTube Dislike - returnyoutubedislike.com - Brings back the dislike count on YouTube videos. Uses a combination of archived data and crowd-sourcing. Small quality-of-life extension.
i

Note on uBlock Origin and Chrome/Edge: Google has been phasing out the extension API (Manifest V2) that makes uBlock Origin fully effective. On Chrome and Edge, uBlock Origin will eventually be limited. On Firefox, it will continue to work at full power. This is one more reason to use Firefox.
